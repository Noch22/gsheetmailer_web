Most outreach campaigns fail because they stop after one email. A simple follow-up sequence usually doubles your chances of getting a reply.

You can run that sequence with Google Sheets and Gmail, without adding a heavy CRM.

## A simple 3-step framework

1. Day 0: first personalized email.
2. Day 3: short reminder with extra value.
3. Day 7: final polite follow-up.

Keep each email short and focused on one idea.

## Suggested sheet columns

```
Email | FirstName | Company | Stage | LastSent | Status
```

Use `Stage` to know which template to send next, and `Status` to stop follow-ups when someone replies.

## Example follow-up message

```
Subject: Quick follow-up, {{FirstName}}

Hi {{FirstName}},

Just following up on my previous note.
If helpful, I can share a short example relevant to {{Company}}.

Would that be useful?
```

## Mistakes to avoid

- Sending follow-ups too quickly.
- Repeating the same exact copy.
- Writing long paragraphs.
- Forgetting to track replies.

## Conclusion

A lightweight follow-up sequence is enough to improve results significantly. The key is consistency, not complexity.
