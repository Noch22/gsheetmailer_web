Testimonials build trust and reduce friction in the buying process. The key is asking the right customers at the right moment with a simple message.

## Step 1: identify ideal contacts

In your sheet, track `Email`, `FirstName`, `CustomerType`, `SuccessMoment`, and `TestimonialStatus`.

Focus on recent positive outcomes.

## Step 2: send a concise ask

Subject: Would you share a short testimonial, {{FirstName}}?

Hi {{FirstName}},

We are glad your experience with us has been positive.

If you are open to it, could you share a short testimonial we can use as customer feedback?

## Step 3: send one polite reminder

If there is no response, send one gentle reminder after 5 to 7 days. Keep it short and appreciative.

## Checklist

- Ask after a positive milestone
- Personalize the request context
- Keep ask short and specific
- Limit reminders to one follow-up
- Track completion in your sheet

## Mistakes to avoid

- Generic and impersonal wording
- Multiple reminders
- Asking customers with unresolved issues
- No acknowledgement after receiving a testimonial

## FAQ

### When is the best time to ask for a testimonial?
Right after a clear success result.

### Should I provide a format?
Yes, a simple structure can help customers answer faster.

### Can I batch these requests monthly?
Yes, monthly batches work well for many teams.
